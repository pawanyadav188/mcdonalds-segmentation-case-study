📘 Project Overview

The Electric Vehicle (EV) industry in India is undergoing rapid transformation, driven by rising fuel prices, increased environmental consciousness, government incentives, and rapid technological improvements in EV infrastructure and battery systems. While the market potential is significant, new entrants—especially startups—face a critical challenge: identifying which EV segment (vehicle type and customer type) holds the highest potential for early success.

This project is undertaken to help a hypothetical EV startup make a data-driven strategic decision about:

  1. Which type of EV vehicle (e.g., e-scooter, electric car, delivery van) they should manufacture
  2. Which customer segment they should target in the early market

To approach this problem systematically, the project follows a two-stage analysis framework:

  • Stage 1: Business Question Formulation
    Based on client requirements, we define core business questions, such as:
    What type of EV should the company build? and Who is most likely to adopt it in the early market?

  • Stage 2: Data Collection and Segmentation Analysis
    To answer these questions, the project uses two separate datasets:

    • Dataset 1: Focused on EV vehicle type preference in India (e.g., scooter vs car vs van), helping identify the segment with the highest demand and profit potential.
    • Dataset 2: Focused on customer segmentation using demographic and geographic variables (e.g., age, income, city, region), helping to define the ideal early adopter profile.

The project includes both coding and non-coding components, executed using Python in Google Colab. The methodology combines logical estimation and machine learning with business strategy, and includes:

  • Logical Fermi Estimation to estimate early market size
  • Data collection from government portals, open data sources, and market reports
  • Data preprocessing using Python libraries such as pandas, sklearn, and seaborn
  • Clustering analysis (K-Means) to extract customer segments
  • Graphical visualization of data and clusters
  • Profiling and interpretation of segments
  • Development of a market entry strategy using the 4Ps framework: Product, Price, Place, Promotion

This analysis concludes with:

  • The business context and core questions
  • Sources of data and processing techniques
  • The models, functions, and libraries used
  • Insights from visualizations and segmentation
  • A final, actionable recommendation for the EV startup

By combining quantitative methods with strategic reasoning, this project aims to support a data-informed decision on the most optimal vehicle and customer segment to target in India’s growing EV market.

🔍 Step 1: Client Requirements & Formulated Business Questions

The client is an early-stage Electric Vehicle (EV) startup that is exploring the Indian market. Their goal is to identify the most promising product segment and define a clear target customer base to ensure a strong and focused market entry.

They are seeking data-driven answers to the following key questions:

🔧 Business Questions:

  1. Which type of Electric Vehicle (EV) — such as e-scooter, electric car, or commercial van — shows the highest demand and profit potential in the current Indian market?
  2. Which customer segment (based on age, income, location, etc.) is most likely to adopt this EV type in the early stages?
  3. Which city or region in India is best suited for early market entry, based on EV awareness, infrastructure, and consumer behavior?
  4. What pricing range is most acceptable for the selected target segment, considering affordability and government incentives?
  5. How should the company position and promote the EV product to best connect with its early adopter audience?

These questions form the foundation for the next steps of data collection, analysis, segmentation, and final strategic recommendation.

📂 Step 2: Data Collection & Sources

To answer the formulated business questions from Step 1, five datasets and reports were used in this project. Each one supports different aspects of the analysis — from identifying which EV type is most profitable, to segmenting customers by region and infrastructure readiness.

📘 Dataset 1: EV Category-Wise Sales Data (FY 2024–25)

  • Purpose: To determine which EV type (e.g., scooter, car, commercial) has the highest demand.
  • Source: EVreporter India EV Report – FY 2024–25 (PDF)
  • What it contains:
    • Total EVs sold: 20,37,831
    • E-2Ws sold: 12,09,772 (≈ 60% of total EV sales)
    • E-4Ws sold: 1,15,800
    • EV penetration by category and state
  • Why it's important: Establishes electric scooters (2Ws) as the most in-demand vehicle type, with Karnataka ranking 2nd in national 2W EV sales.

📘 Dataset 2: State-Wise EV Registration Volumes (Geographic Distribution)

  • Purpose: To understand which states have the highest EV usage in absolute numbers.
  • Source: State/UT-wise Total EV Registrations – data.gov.in
  • What it contains:
    • Number of EVs registered in each state/UT
    • State-wise EV penetration vs total vehicles
  • Why it's important: Identifies regions with high EV usage, which are likely to accept new EV launches quickly. Karnataka shows both high volume and high percentage of EVs in total vehicle population.

📘 Dataset 3: Public EV Charging Infrastructure (State-wise)

  • Purpose: To evaluate infrastructure readiness to support EV adoption.
  • Source: State/UT-wise Public EV Charging Stations – data.gov.in
  • What it contains:
    • Number of operational public charging stations in each state
  • Key Insight: Karnataka leads with 5,765 stations, more than Maharashtra (3,728) and Delhi (1,941).
  • Why it's important: Regions with high EV infrastructure offer a better customer experience and are ideal for early market entry.

📘 Dataset 4: Geographic EV Registration Breakdown (Urban vs Rural)

  • Purpose: To assess whether EV adoption is higher in urban areas (e.g., Bangalore) vs rural areas, across different states.
  • Source: EVreporter India EV Report – FY 2024–25 (PDF)
  • What it contains:
    • State-wise and city-wise EV registration shares
    • Urban-focused EV adoption patterns
  • Why it's important: Helps identify not just which states, but which specific cities or clusters are EV ready. Bangalore stands out for both adoption and charging network density.

📘 Dataset 5: Upcoming Infrastructure Developments (News Reports)

  • Purpose: To include future plans for EV infrastructure expansion.
  • Sources:
    • Kolkata EV infra expansion – TOI
    • Assam to add chargers in all towns
  • What it contains:
    • News-based updates on planned charging stations at the city level
  • Why it's important: Provides a forward-looking lens to evaluate which areas are improving rapidly in EV infrastructure.

🧠 Summary of Key Data Insights:

Metric Insight
Most sold EV category 🛵 Electric 2-wheelers (12 lakh units)
Best state by EV adoption 🔋 Karnataka (11.3% 2W EV penetration)
Most charging stations ⚡ Karnataka (5,765 stations)
Best launch city 🌆 Bangalore (population ~20 million, high EV use)

🧠 Step 3: Fermi Estimation – Estimating Early Market Size for E-Scooters in Karnataka

Fermi Estimation is a method to logically break down a market size using basic assumptions, especially when full data isn’t available. Based on the FY 2024–25 EVreporter report and government datasets, this step estimates how many people in Karnataka (especially Bangalore) are likely to adopt e-scooters in the early market.

📈 Why Focus on Electric Scooters (E-2W)?

As per the EVreporter FY 2024–25 report:

  • 12,09,772 electric two-wheelers (E-2W) were sold in India — making up 59.4% of total EV sales.
  • E-scooters far outperformed EV cars (1,15,800 units).
  • Two-wheelers dominate urban India due to low cost, personal convenience, and daily commuting needs.
  • Government subsidies and rising fuel prices are further boosting demand.

This confirms that e-scooters are the most commercially viable EV product for mass adoption.

📍 Why Karnataka?

From combined data:

  • Karnataka is the 2nd highest state in E-2W sales (1,48,304 units sold).
  • EV penetration in Karnataka’s 2W segment is 11.3%, compared to national avg of 6.2%.
  • Karnataka has India’s largest EV charging network with 5,765 public charging stations.
  • Bangalore, the capital, is a tech hub with high population density, daily commuters, and early adopters.

All signs point to Karnataka — particularly Bangalore — as the most suitable early market for EV launch.

🧮 Fermi Estimation – Market Size in Bangalore

Parameter Estimate Justification
Total population of Bangalore 20 million 2024 estimate
% aged 18–45 (working segment) 45% → 9 million Commuting age group
% who commute daily 60% → 5.4 million Urban mobility users
% with commute < 30 km/day 65% → 3.5 million Ideal range for e-scooters
% interested in EVs (early adopters) 10% → 3,50,000 Based on urban awareness
% who can afford e-scooter this year 5% → 17,500 buyers ~₹90,000–₹1,00,000 price range

✅ Final Estimate:

Based on Fermi logic, 17,000 to 18,000 people in Bangalore are realistic e-scooter buyers in the next 12 months.

This number can easily grow to 40,000+ buyers across Karnataka by including Tier-2 cities like Mysuru, Mangaluru, and Hubballi.

🎯 Business Relevance:

This estimation supports the startup’s decision to:

  • Choose electric scooters over other EV types
  • Target Karnataka, a state with:
    • High adoption rate
    • Strong EV infrastructure
    • Favorable urban demographics
  • Forecast an achievable early market size to plan production and pricing

It forms the foundation for customer segmentation and strategy development in the next steps.

🧹 Step 4: Data Preprocessing

Before applying any analysis or machine learning models, it is crucial to clean and prepare the datasets to ensure accuracy and consistency. This step outlines the basic preprocessing steps performed on the three key datasets used in this project.

🔹 1. EV Category-Wise Dataset (Sales by Type)

This dataset contains electric vehicle sales data broken down by vehicle category (2W, 3W, 4W, buses, commercial vans, etc.) for the years 2022–23 and 2023–24.

Preprocessing steps:

  • Renamed column headers for clarity (e.g., "Vehicle Type" → "EV_Category")
  • Converted numeric columns like "Units Sold" to integer type
  • Removed rows with aggregated totals or null values
  • Checked for duplicate entries and dropped if found
  • Added a “Growth %” column to calculate YoY percentage increase

🔹 2. State-Wise EV Registration Dataset

This dataset shows the total number of EVs registered in each Indian state, as well as total vehicle count and EV share (%).

Preprocessing steps:

  • Standardized state names (some states had inconsistent casing or extra spaces)
  • Converted string percentages like "4.3%" to float (e.g., 4.3)
  • Handled missing values using median imputation for any incomplete state rows
  • Filtered to keep only latest year data (FY 2023–24)
  • Sorted the dataframe by EV share % for plotting later

🔹 3. Public Charging Station Dataset

This dataset lists the number of operational public EV charging stations in each state as of March 2024.

Preprocessing steps:

  • Checked for and removed rows with null or “N/A” charging station counts
  • Converted "Number of Stations" column to numeric
  • Merged this dataset later with state-wise registration data for comparative visuals

🧩 Step 7: Segment Profiling – Understanding the Clusters


After applying K-Means clustering using two geographic variables — EV Share (%) and Number of Charging Stations — the states were grouped into three distinct clusters.


These clusters represent states that are similar in their readiness for electric vehicles, helping the startup identify where to enter first, where to monitor, and where to avoid initially.




🔷 Cluster 0 – EV Ready Leaders


Traits:


    

  • High EV share in total vehicle sales
    • 

  • Strong charging infrastructure
    • 

  • Includes urban, tech-friendly states with early adopters
    • 



Example States: Karnataka, Maharashtra, Delhi


Business Strategy:


    

  • These are the best regions for initial product launch
    • 

  • Focus marketing, partnerships, and delivery networks here
    • 





🟢 Cluster 1 – Growing but Under-Infra


Traits:


    

  • Good EV adoption rate
    • 

  • Limited charging stations (infrastructure still developing)
    • 

  • Urbanizing Tier 2 states with policy push
    • 



Example States: Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Rajasthan


Business Strategy:


    

  • Secondary targets for expansion
    • 

  • May benefit from government infrastructure support soon
    • 





🔴 Cluster 2 – EV Laggards


Traits:


    

  • Low EV adoption
    • 

  • Low infrastructure
    • 

  • Smaller or rural-dominated states
    • 



Example States: Bihar, Assam, Jharkhand


Business Strategy:


    

  • Avoid during early-stage launch
    • 

  • Consider for future rollout after awareness and infra improve
    • 





🧠 Summary:


Clustering helped uncover clear regional differences in EV readiness:


    

  • Cluster 0 is your early market
    • 

  • Cluster 1 is your next opportunity
    • 

  • Cluster 2 is a watchlist for future expansion
    • 



These insights directly support the go-to-market plan in Step 8.

    •

    • 

  • Additional Edge: Lower manufacturing cost compared to EV cars; less dependency on public charging infra.
    •
